# The Wheel

The centrepiece: a retro game-show wheel that lets the diner *feel* in control while
the house quietly decides the outcome. It can be spun two ways, and it always lands
with maximum drama.

## Two ways to spin

**Drag-to-flick.** Grab the wheel and throw it. While dragging, the wheel follows the
cursor 1:1 around its hub and ratchets a tick on every slice boundary. On release the
flick's speed and direction are measured:

- The harder you flick, the more revolutions of suspense (between **2 and 8**).
- The spin launches at *exactly* your release speed, so there's no visible snap between
  your hand and the animation — it reads as real momentum.
- A **too-gentle flick** doesn't spin: the wheel wobbles back to where it started, a sad
  trombone plays, and a rotating taunt appears ("Weak wrist. The house is unimpressed.").

**The SPIN button.** Prefer a classic casino throw? The centre button spins clockwise
every time, long and loud — **5 to 8** revolutions over roughly **5–7 seconds** of
agonising slow-down.

A first-visit hint ("grab & flick — or smash SPIN") points the way and hides itself
once you've spun.

## The rigged part (the whole point)

Whichever way you spin, the **winner is chosen the instant you commit** — a weighted
random pick where each slice's odds match its size. The flick only buys *direction and
drama*, never the result. Then the wheel is told to land on a pre-computed angle that
stops **just inside the winner's edge** (1–2.5°), as if it barely escaped the
neighbouring slice. Every single spin looks like a near-miss. That's deliberate — it's
the oldest trick in the slot-machine book.

## The cuisine → restaurant flip

If the wheel was a **cuisine** wheel, landing isn't the end. The winning cuisine is
announced, then the wheel does a 3D flip and reloads with up to **15** of that cuisine's
nearest restaurants (equal odds). The **second** spin is final. So "by food mood" is a
two-act drama: *what kind of food* → *which exact place*.

A **restaurant** wheel (chosen directly, or reached via the flip) lands once and that's
dinner.

## Sights and sounds while it spins

- Marquee bulbs around the rim blink in a frenzy during the spin.
- Ticks accelerate and slow with the wheel; goofy spin music tracks its tempo.
- A confetti burst fires the instant it lands; a fanfare plays the winner home.
